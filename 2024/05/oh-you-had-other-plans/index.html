Oh, you had other plans?

Disclaimer: Made up. Especially the people and their names.

There’s this thing you wanted to do for quite some time now. A hike across some pass in the Alps with a bit of via ferrata? You have the route figured out, the forecast is promising, the weather in the past weeks was just right (there won’t be snow, avalanches, mud), you have the holidays booked, no last minute fire at work. Last year the plan fell flat because of a mudslide that made the valley inaccessible, the year before you caught Covid, next year you’re already booked out, and this year it’s late in the year and not many potential weekends left.

You have invited a few friends with whom you’ve climbed mountain passes before.

And then

“Is it fine to invite Eddie?”

Eddie is cool and you don’t want to be exclusionary. So Eddie joins. And Eddie likes the plan … but is far not as excited about it as you are - as you’ll later learn - and wouldn’t mind doing anything else. And so Eddie asks if they should bring Joey along … okay … Joey joins … and Joey is happy to do something on the coming weekend and asks if people already have suggestions what to do (he doesn’t know what he signed up for?).

The thing is, Joey doesn’t have a via ferrata kit and no experience and the path isn’t that easy that you could bring a beginner along. So the plan gets changed. Instead of crossing the pass, it will stay in one of the valley and turn into a 20k hike.

Until Joey mentions that he invited Carla and Mike. They had already thought for quite a while about using a sunny weekend to do a 2h - wait! what’s happening? - hike in the mountains and suggest to cut the loop down to 1/3 and then do a barbecue. People are already organizing the grill and food.

And since Freddy and Tom (where did they come from?) have a bit of a scheduling conflict, it’s now first barbecue and then hike.

Surely, you’re at the grilling place at 11 as agreed. And at 14 the first round of food is eaten and you mentally prepare for packing (wondering a bit how everybody else behaves as if they have no more plans for the rest of the day) when Martin and Constantine finally shows up and reignites the grill - does he want to go on the hike with flip-flops? And by 16 people are chatting, and someone mentions hiking and everybody agrees that hiking is actually a nice activity and would’ve been a nice alternative, Martin looks at you and says that you even have proper shoes to go on a hike, though he always thought of you to be more a via ferrata person and starting for a hike that late in the day would probably be too short for your preferences.

Strangling everybody around you won’t turn the clocks back to 8 and won’t get your equipment here. Next try in two years, try not to get injured, a job in a new place, and start looking for a new group to do the tour with.
